# This Wiki

## Summary

This wiki is a personal intellectual reference work. It is designed to collect, organize, clarify, and extend a large body of interests that span mathematics, computer science, philosophy, language, history, symbolic systems, and technical practice. It serves both as a repository of knowledge and as a framework for ongoing thought.

Unlike a generic blog or a conventional personal website, this wiki is not primarily a place for self-presentation, status display, or chronological posting. Its central purpose is intellectual organization. It exists to preserve concepts, definitions, distinctions, problems, traditions, and relationships between ideas in a form that can be browsed, expanded, revised, and linked over time.

This wiki is also shaped by the use of artificial intelligence. AI is used here as an assistant for drafting, clarifying, summarizing, reorganizing, and connecting ideas. However, the wiki is not intended to function as an unfiltered dump of machine-generated text. It remains a curated knowledge system organized around personal judgment, selection, and long-term conceptual interest.

## Purpose

The purpose of this wiki is to create a stable and extensible intellectual environment for serious study.

Many forms of digital writing are poorly suited for this goal. Social media is immediate and fragmented. Blogs are often chronological and difficult to reorganize. Notes apps can become private piles of disconnected fragments. Academic writing is often too formal and slow for exploratory thinking. Conventional encyclopedias are broad but impersonal, and they usually do not reflect a particular person's conceptual priorities.

This wiki attempts to solve a different problem: how to build a personal knowledge structure that is cumulative, revisable, cross-linked, and intellectually serious.

Its basic aims are:

- to preserve important ideas in durable written form
    
- to clarify concepts through explanation
    
- to relate isolated topics to a larger conceptual network
    
- to create a personal map of fields, problems, and traditions
    
- to support future writing, research, software projects, and study
    

For these reasons, the wiki is not simply a notebook. It is closer to a personal encyclopedia, a research scaffold, and a conceptual index.

## Why a wiki format

The wiki form is especially suited to this project because it treats knowledge as a network rather than a sequence.

Chronological formats such as blogs encourage forward motion. New entries replace old attention. By contrast, a wiki allows old material to remain active. A page written long ago can still function as a node in a living structure of concepts.

This has several advantages.

First, it allows gradual refinement. A page does not need to be perfect at the moment of creation. It can begin as a short entry and later become a substantial article.

Second, it encourages cross-linking. A concept can be approached from many directions. A page on [[Fourier transform]] can be linked from [[Analysis]], [[Signal processing]], [[Quantum mechanics]], or [[Representation theory]].

Third, it supports non-linear study. A reader can move by concept, by field, by historical lineage, or by technical dependency.

Fourth, it permits coexistence between mature and immature knowledge. Some pages will be polished overviews. Others will be rough conceptual placeholders waiting for expansion.

This matches the actual growth of understanding more closely than a finished-book model does.

## Personal view of knowledge

This wiki is based on a particular view of knowledge.

Knowledge is not treated here as a heap of isolated facts. It is treated as structured understanding. A fact matters most when it is placed inside a system of relations: what it depends on, what it implies, what it resembles, what problem it solves, and what larger tradition it belongs to.

For that reason, this wiki places high value on:

- definition
    
- distinction
    
- hierarchy
    
- linkage
    
- conceptual role
    
- historical placement
    
- explanatory clarity
    

This view rejects two common extremes.

The first extreme is mere accumulation. One can gather huge amounts of information without building understanding. A pile of excerpts, bookmarks, quotations, and copied notes is not yet knowledge in the stronger sense.

The second extreme is empty simplification. One can also produce elegant summaries that remove all technical content and therefore explain very little.

This wiki aims at a middle path: technically serious, but still readable; conceptually ambitious, but still organized; selective, but cumulative.

Knowledge, on this view, is not just possession of information. It is the ability to situate an idea in a larger field of meaning.

## Personal view of wikis

A wiki is often treated as either a public encyclopedia or a casual note-taking tool. This project takes a different view.

A wiki can function as a **private intellectual infrastructure**.

That means it is not only a place to store finished conclusions. It is also a place to:

- stage ongoing interpretation
    
- mark unresolved problems
    
- connect distant fields
    
- define terms consistently
    
- preserve conceptual discoveries before they are forgotten
    
- prepare material for future essays, software, or research
    

Under this view, a wiki is not merely archival. It is generative. It helps produce thought by giving thought a stable surface on which to accumulate and reorganize itself.

A good wiki does not only answer questions. It makes better questions possible.

## Use of AI

Artificial intelligence is used in this wiki as a tool for augmentation rather than replacement.

AI can be useful in many parts of the process:

- drafting first-pass articles
    
- explaining technical ideas in simpler language
    
- generating outlines
    
- comparing related concepts
    
- identifying missing links
    
- rephrasing obscure passages
    
- converting scattered notes into coherent prose
    
- suggesting classifications and article structures
    

This is especially useful in a large personal wiki, because the number of possible pages, links, summaries, and conceptual cross-references quickly becomes too large to handle efficiently by memory alone.

However, the use of AI introduces risks.

AI systems can produce plausible but inaccurate statements. They can flatten distinctions, erase uncertainty, or present conventional summaries where a more careful treatment is needed. They can also generate text that looks polished while lacking depth or fidelity.

For this reason, AI-generated or AI-assisted material in this wiki should be understood as subject to curation, correction, and revision. AI helps accelerate expression and organization, but it does not replace judgment.

The guiding principle is that AI is a **force multiplier for intellectual work**, not an authority independent of it.

## AI as an enhancement to personal thought

The use of AI here reflects a broader belief: intellectual tools matter because they extend what a single mind can stabilize, revisit, and develop.

Writing already performs this function. So do diagrams, symbolic notations, libraries, indexes, databases, and archives. AI belongs to the same general family of cognitive tools, though it has a different character. It can respond dynamically, generate variants, summarize large contexts, and help transform rough conceptual material into more structured prose.

In this wiki, AI is valuable when it helps do the following:

- move from vague intuition to explicit formulation
    
- convert raw notes into article form
    
- widen the local neighborhood of a concept
    
- preserve momentum during large research projects
    
- expose forgotten assumptions or missing definitions
    
- maintain stylistic consistency across many pages
    

This is particularly helpful in technical and philosophical subjects where the main difficulty is often not lack of information, but difficulty in organizing and expressing it clearly.

## Style of the wiki

The style of this wiki differs from both casual blogging and conventional institutional encyclopedias.

It is intended to be:

- dry in tone
    
- clear in structure
    
- serious in substance
    
- readable by a general educated reader
    
- careful with definitions
    
- generous with conceptual explanation
    
- rich in internal links
    

Technical concepts are explained in plain language wherever possible. This does not mean avoiding technical vocabulary entirely. It means explaining such vocabulary rather than treating it as self-interpreting.

The aim is not to simplify a subject into emptiness, but to make it intelligible without sacrificing its structure.

In this respect, the wiki prefers:

- explanation over display
    
- conceptual role over rote terminology
    
- structural understanding over disconnected facts
    
- linked knowledge over isolated entries
    

## Why this is personal rather than neutral

No personal wiki can be fully neutral, because selection itself is interpretive.

What is included, what is omitted, what receives long treatment, and what receives only a stub all reflect judgments about value, relevance, and conceptual importance.

This wiki does not pretend otherwise. It is personal in at least four senses.

First, it reflects a particular pattern of interests.

Second, it reflects a particular intellectual temperament, one that values abstraction, structural explanation, formal systems, deep linkage, and conceptual clarity.

Third, it reflects a particular method of study, in which reading, software work, note-taking, and long-term synthesis are tightly connected.

Fourth, it reflects a particular ambition: not merely to consume knowledge, but to build a coherent and reusable internal world of it.

This personal orientation is not a weakness. It is what gives the wiki coherence.

## Relation to reading, research, and software

This wiki stands between several activities that are often kept separate.

It is related to reading, because many pages originate in books, papers, lectures, and long-term study.

It is related to research, because it preserves questions, distinctions, and frameworks that may later develop into essays or more formal inquiry.

It is related to software, because many topics here concern computation, systems, symbolic representation, formal language, and information structure.

It is therefore best understood not as a finished publication, but as an intermediate intellectual layer between raw input and more polished output.

A book may feed the wiki. The wiki may feed an essay. An essay may feed a program. A program may reveal a new conceptual problem, which returns to the wiki.

## Long-term ambition

The long-term ambition of this wiki is cumulative clarity.

This means building a body of knowledge that becomes more useful over time rather than less. Many digital systems decay into clutter. This project aims at the opposite. The hope is that repeated revision, linking, and conceptual consolidation will make the whole structure increasingly navigable and increasingly expressive.

Over time, this wiki may grow to include:

- foundational overview pages
    
- field summaries
    
- concept articles
    
- historical tradition pages
    
- profiles of thinkers
    
- technical notes
    
- reading syntheses
    
- ontological classifications
    
- project documentation
    
- unresolved questions and conjectural ideas
    

In this way, the wiki serves both preservation and construction. It records what has been learned, but it also helps shape what can be learned next.
